Q. What is middleware?

A. Middleware is application-level software that extends or abstracts the native operating system services. If it solely abstracts and doesn't add significant functionality, it is called cross-platform support middleware.

Q. What are the tradeoffs involved in deciding whether or not to use middleware?

A. The major benefits include increased portability and added functionality. Risks include cost, performance loss due to additional software layers, and the inability to take advantage of platform-specific features if these are hidden by the middleware.
